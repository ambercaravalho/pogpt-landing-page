Project Purpose

Demonstrate the capability of Large Language Models (LLMs) in analyzing, summarizing, and deriving insights from Controlled, Classified, Personally Identifiable Information (PII), and Protected Health Information (PHI) while ensuring data security and confidentiality through local containerized environments. This project aims to explore the potential of LLMs as a tool for handling sensitive information with precision and accuracy, maintaining compliance with relevant regulations.

Relevance To The DoD

The integration of multiple open-source projects within various DoD networks exemplifies our commitment to leveraging cutting-edge technologies to fortify national defense capabilities. Together, these technologies provide the DoD with a robust platform for data-driven decision-making, operational efficiency, and enhanced security measures, ensuring that our defense mechanisms remain adaptable and resilient in the face of evolving threats.

Use Cases

  • Data Analysis and Insight Generation: Leverage Large Language Models to sift through extensive Air Force operational data, identifying patterns, insights, and actionable intelligence that support decision-making processes.
  • Automated Report Generation: Utilize the technology to automatically generate comprehensive reports on network performance, security incidents, and maintenance needs, significantly reducing manual workload and enhancing operational efficiency.
  • Enhanced Communication: Implement natural language processing to improve internal communication platforms, enabling more efficient query handling, information retrieval, and automated assistance for personnel across Air Force networks.
  • Training and Simulation: Apply LLMs to develop sophisticated training modules and simulations, offering realistic and dynamic learning environments for personnel to hone their skills in cybersecurity, network management, and other critical areas.
  • Security and Threat Detection: Deploy models to monitor network traffic in real-time, detect anomalies, and identify potential security threats with high precision, facilitating rapid response and mitigation strategies to protect sensitive Air Force infrastructure.

Technologies In Use

Docker: Facilitates the deployment of our applications across any network, ensuring consistency and simplifying maintenance. Docker containers package the application and its dependencies into a single unit, promoting portability and scalability essential for Air Force operations.

Open WebUI: Serves as the intuitive front-end interface for interacting with Large Language Models (LLMs). It simplifies the user experience, allowing personnel to easily access, query, and utilize LLM capabilities for data analysis, report generation, and decision support.

Ollama: Acts as the LLM provider and middleware, bridging the gap between raw computational power and practical applications. Ollama facilitates the processing of natural language queries and the generation of insightful, contextually relevant responses, enhancing operational intelligence and efficiency.

LLaVA: Extends the utility of LLMs by incorporating visual data processing, enabling the system to analyze and interpret imagery alongside textual data. This integration supports a wider range of applications, from reconnaissance data analysis to maintenance and repair tasks, by providing a comprehensive understanding of both textual and visual information.
